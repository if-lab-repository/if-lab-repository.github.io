Workshop Introduction

The Workshop on Processing-In-Memory (PIM) Technology (PIMT) will provide a forum for leading experts in the relevant research thrusts of Processing-In-Memory (PIM) technology, specifically circuit, architecture, systems, and applications. This will enable researchers to brainstorm the latest research progress and discuss their visions of the critical challenges that need to be addressed in the near future. The experts from these four thrust areas should tackle these challenges in a synergistic manner.

The structure of the workshop is that it will be divided into four above-mentioned thrust areas led by four leading experts. The thrust leaders will invite speakers. Other participants will be selected from solicited submissions. The workshop will consist of a series of invited talks by leading experts who will discuss the grand challenges that span the four thrust areas. Some panel discussions will be also included in the program to synthesize the overall theme and provide an opportunity for attendees to participate in the discussion.

We anticipate that the experts will present their views on the grand challenges in each thrust area and the various potential ways to tackle them. We hope PIMT can help the community better understand these grand challenges rather than incremental changes. This free flow of information exchange will enable us to form a stronger synergy among various themes and propose transformative ideas to achieve significant progress in the next decade.

Call for Participations

This workshop will invite speakers and participants from all the research thrusts, especially by those that may not have adequate support from their educational institution and those underrepresented groups in the fields related to the workshop. One of the four thrust leaders is a female researcher, Sharon Hu from Notre Dame University. The workshop organizing committee will actively invite members in underrepresented groups to ensure that at least 20% of the invited speakers are from such groups.

Researchers applying for attending the workshop (except for the invited talks) will be selected for funding based on the following (ranked) criteria: 1. Presenters at the workshop. 2. Lack of any available travel support. 3. Membership in an underrepresented group. During the speaker invitation process, the organizing committee will also give a high priority to members in underrepresented groups.

Researchers that are supported by this grant for attending the workshop will be required to submit a summary of their experience at or right after the workshop. A summary of these experiences will be included in the final report.
